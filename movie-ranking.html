The Best Movies According to Chris

My Top 3 Movies of All Time

Crash

Crash tells several intertwining stories as people interact with one another during their day in California. Just when you think things can't get worse, they do. When you think people maybe aren't so bad. Turns out they aren't. But no, wait, yes they are actually. You may find yourself examining yourself or those around you through a slightly different lens.

WALL-E

Disney's Pixar's WALL-E is an amazing, colorful and possibly prophetic movie regarding the potential course of humanity. The innocence and wonder in WALL-E as he simply exists is something that people can learn from as they age. Imagine all the possibilities of just being.

The 13th Warrior

What I believe is a take on the classic tale of Baewulf and Grendel. Starring Antonio Banderas we watch as he journeys across deserts as an ambassador and ends up being prophesied to help 12 other warriors save a village from an ancient evil. What I really love about the film is that you are immersed in the story as you seemingly sit by the fireside and listen to men tell stories in a foreign language until you are able to pick up on tone and cadence and deduce context and thereby learn. It's very interesting to see translated to film and it helps to explain a language barrier that most films would have chosen to overlook.
